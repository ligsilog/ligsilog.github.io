What is Cannabidiol?
Cannabidiol or cbd is a chemical compound that can be extracted from cannabis plant, commonly known as marijuana or hemp. It is one of the cannabinoids that can be found in the plant.

Because of the negative notions that come with the words “hemp” and “marijuana”, it was only in the recent years when studies and researches have given thought in the positive benefits that the plant can give.

Contrary to popular belief, not everything about marijuana can make you stoned. CBD is a cannabinoid that does not have psychoactive effects. It is extracted from the leaves, flowers, and stalks of the plant which is unlike the common hemp oil in the market which is extracted from the seed.

CBD and THC
Marijuana has been studied for years because of its ability to ease symptoms of anxiety, lessen pain, and stop seizures from occurring.

CBD, although sometimes mistaken as THC, the other cannabinoid found in hemp, are very different from each other. THC or tetrahydrocannabinol is responsible for the “high” in marijuana while CBD is much safer and have more benefits.

CBD and THC both affect the endocannabinoid system but in different ways.
The endocannabinoid system was discovered during studies of how cannabis affects our body, and surprisingly, that is how it got its name since it means that our body naturally does process and produce cannabinoids.

The endocannabinoid system mainly affects a person’s mood, appetite, memory, and pain-sensation. THC binds directly to the cannabinoid receptors of the system which results to the feeling of being “high”.

On the contrary, CBD still stimulates the cb receptors but is done indirectly thus, reaping the benefits without being stoned.

Cannabidiol oil and Anxiety disorders
Unlike most antidepressant drugs, cbd oil reduces risk for suicide and helps improve sleep.

There are now a lot of studies that strongly supports that cbd can be used as a form of treatment for anxiety disorders such as PTSD or post-traumatic stress disorder, general anxiety disorder, social anxiety disorder, OCD or obsessive-compulsive disorder, and panic disorder.

In a study done in 2011, 24 patients who have social anxiety disorder and never been treated were tested. One set were given cbd while the other set is given placebo. They don’t know if they were given the placebo or the cbd.

As the results have shown, after undergoing simulation public speaking test (SPST) the set of people who were given the placebo were significantly more anxious than the set of people who received cbd treatment.

This is just one of the studies that connect the intake of cbd and lessened anxiety symptoms.
